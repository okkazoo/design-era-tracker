What to do

Your task is to explore how a specific group is represented (as a stereotype) in horror films. You can choose a different group and medium if you get approval from your teacher.

You will do this by:

  • Showing how media texts represent the group.
  • Describing the features of the media texts that create this representation.
  • Providing specific evidence from the texts.
  • Explaining the effect of the representation.

This is a two-part assessment. You need to complete a visual diary and then present your findings either as a narrated visual sequence, a written report, or by submitting your visual diary notes directly.

Key requirements

  • Achievement:
  • Select at least three media texts that represent your chosen group.
  • Describe how the media texts represent the group.
  • Describe the features of the media texts that create the representation.
  • Describe the effect of the media representation, using evidence.
  • Merit: All Achievement requirements, plus:
  • Describe reasoned explanations for the *effect(s)* of the representation.
  • Explain reasons for differences between the representation and reality.
  • Explain reasons for the stereotypes, messages, or values created.
  • Excellence: All Achieved and Merit requirements, plus:
  • Examine the likely consequences of the representation.
  • Draw conclusions about the media's presentation of the group, including implications of differences from reality, effectiveness of stereotypes, and issues related to omissions or creator's worldview.

You must answer the first 5 questions (yellow highlighted) for each text to achieve.

Tips

  • Use your visual diary to keep notes, videos, pictures, screenshots, and links.
  • Focus your notes on the provided questions, but feel free to add your own ideas.
  • Some questions may overlap; you don't need to answer them identically multiple times.
  • You do not need to answer all Merit or Excellence questions to achieve those grades, but you must answer all Achieved questions.
  • For your final presentation, ensure your visuals (screenshots) support your discussion.
  • Your visual diary is mandatory for everyone.
  • Consider research from interviews with actors/directors, critic reviews, and statistics.
