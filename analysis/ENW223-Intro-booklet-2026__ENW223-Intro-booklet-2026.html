What to do

  • This course is for students who enjoy writing and want to improve their skills.
  • You will read texts to inspire your own writing.
  • You will cover different types of writing like poetry, short stories, reviews, and essays.
  • You will do writing exercises throughout the year.
  • You will get to do your own investigation into an aspect of writing you are interested in.
  • You will learn to analyze and interpret literature, moving beyond just describing it.
  • You will examine texts closely and ask questions about the author's choices.
  • You will develop your ability to analyze how literature engages audiences and use aspects like language, point of view, and structure in your own writing.

Key requirements

  • You will need pens and an exercise book for notes and writing exercises.
  • Bring your own device (computer) to class.
  • All assessments will be given feedback through Google Classroom.
  • You must read and understand the NZQA rules about assessment opportunities.
  • You need to be aware of targets and deadlines for assessments.
  • If you have problems meeting a deadline, discuss it with your teacher early.
  • Pay attention to all feedback and use it to improve your work.
  • You need to be prepared for external exams by completing formative tasks and achieving good grades in practice exams.
  • Keep notes on texts studied in class to help prepare for exams.

Tips

  • REAL ANALYSIS MAKES AN ARGUABLE CLAIM.
  • Disagreements about interpretation are important for discussion and help you make stronger arguments or realize when your analysis needs improvement.
  • To be eligible for external exams, you need to demonstrate preparedness.
