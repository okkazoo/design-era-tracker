What to do

  • Choose a design movement or era that interests you.
  • Research the chosen movement, focusing on its characteristics, key designers, and social/cultural influences.
  • Create a visual presentation about your chosen movement.
  • Use the design elements and principles of that movement to inform your own design ideas for a garment (shirt/top).
  • Develop your design ideas through drawing, refining them, and creating a final design.
  • Produce a working drawing showing construction details for your final design.
  • Write your work in your own words, proof-read it, and include a list of sources.

Key requirements

  • Research Presentation: An A3 visual presentation (max 3 pages) about your chosen design movement, including its characteristics, relevant social factors, and examples.
  • Design Ideas: Develop design ideas for a shirt/top that clearly show the influence of your chosen design movement, using ideation techniques and drawing.
  • Working Drawing: A detailed line drawing of your final design, showing construction details and any adaptations.
  • Final Design Page: A rendered drawing of your final design on a body, with notes on fabric and notions, linking back to your inspiration.
  • Written Work: Must be in your own words, proof-read, and include a bibliography.
  • Attribution: All images must be labelled with the design, designer, and year. Citations and a reference list are required.

Tips

  • Discuss your chosen design movement with BKJ before starting research.
  • Use the Design Era Research Notes worksheet to gather and organize information.
  • Be selective with images and information for your presentation.
  • Use ideation techniques like tessellating, stretching/shortening, bending, layering, and adding depth to explore shapes.
  • Make sure your design ideas clearly show links to your ideation through repetition of lines, shapes, textures, colours, and forms.
  • Your written work must be proof-read for spelling and grammar.
